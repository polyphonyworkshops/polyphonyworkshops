Music Theory

Music Theory workshop is an excellent opportunity to cover and practice all the music theory that students do not have time to cover during their lessons in a fun and collective way.
With the use of interactive games and Kahoot quizzes, students will dive into the syllabus of each grade of music theory and prepare for any future music theory exams.

How to Improve your Aural Skills

This workshop aims to help students build their confidence around aural tests.
Through specialized activities, we will practice Sight-singing.
We will warm up our voice, learn how to sing specific intervals, and finally sight-sing a small melody.
In this workshop, we will also explore different expressions we can use to describe the character of music as well as recognizing the music period that it belongs to.
Based on the ABRSM Syllabus.

How to Improve your Scales

Different methods to help students to memorize their scales efficiently and be prepared and confident for their exams.
I will be sharing tips and methods of practicing scales in a way that would make them memorable forever.
These methods include technical approaches for practicing such as fingering, speed, and articulation of a scale, and also music theory that will set the fundamentals for memorizing and being able to remember the structure of each scale.

Coding for Young Musicians

With the use of the web-based platform "Scratch", the educational tool for young coders, the students will get an introduction to creative programming with a special focus on music. During this workshop, we will learn how to code with Scratch by making two musical apps. The "virtual piano" and the "alternative orchestra" will introduce students to the relation of music and computers and how this relationship can be a creative expression as well as a helpful tool for understanding music and musical instruments.
No prior knowledge of programming needed.
